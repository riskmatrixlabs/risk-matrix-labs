## Brand Personality

If Risk Matrix Labs were a person, they would be a former trader who now bets seriously. They are disciplined, data-driven, and direct. They don't hype picks or chase dopamine. They operate a system, track everything, and let the numbers do the talking. They respect you enough to tell you the truth — even when it means saying "you're tilted, don't bet tonight."

## Voice Attributes

### 1. Disciplined
- **We are:** Systematic, process-focused, consistent. Every message reinforces the idea that discipline — not luck — is the edge.
- **We are not:** Preachy, moralistic, or lecture-y. We don't shame gamblers. We show them a better way.
- **This sounds like:** "Your Discipline Score™ doesn't lie. Grade your process — not your results."
- **This does NOT sound like:** "You really need to stop gambling and start being responsible."

### 2. Direct
- **We are:** Punchy, no-filler, short sentences. We say exactly what we mean.
- **We are not:** Curt or cold. Directness comes with confidence, not rudeness.
- **This sounds like:** "No mental math. No guessing. The system calculates it."
- **This does NOT sound like:** "Our innovative platform leverages cutting-edge technology to provide real-time insights into your betting performance metrics."

### 3. Operator-Coded
- **We are:** We use the language of trading, risk management, and systems — not gambling. Our users are operators, not bettors.
- **We are not:** Finance bros. We're not pretentious. The operator framing is aspirational, not elitist.
- **This sounds like:** "Track every dollar. Manage your exposure. Grade every session."
- **This does NOT sound like:** "We leverage synergistic risk management frameworks to optimize your ROI."

### 4. Credible Without Arrogance
- **We are:** Confident in the product. We don't need to oversell it. We let the features speak.
- **We are not:** Hypey, inflated, or full of superlatives. No "best," "only," "revolutionary" without proof.
- **This sounds like:** "Built because no tool existed that treated sports betting like a risk management discipline."
- **This does NOT sound like:** "The world's #1 sports betting platform trusted by thousands of winners."

### 5. Honest
- **We are:** Transparent about what the product is and isn't. We don't sell picks. We don't promise wins.
- **We are not:** Hedging or vague. Honesty is direct and clear.
- **This sounds like:** "This is about discipline and process — not predictions."
- **This does NOT sound like:** "Results may vary. Past performance is not indicative of future results." (buried legal fine print)

## Audience

### Primary: The Serious Recreational Bettor
- Bets regularly (3–7x/week), takes it seriously
- Frustrated that they can't track their results properly
- Knows what units are, knows what -110 means
- Lost money before and wants to understand why
- **What they need to hear:** "You don't need better picks. You need a better system."

### Secondary: The Capper / Content Creator
- Has an audience of bettors
- Wants credibility and a tool they can authentically recommend
- Cares about their record and reputation
- **What they need to hear:** "Your audience deserves better tools. Refer them. Get paid."

## Style Rules

### DO
- Use sentence case for most headings ("Every tool an operator needs")
- Use ALL CAPS for UI badges, labels, status indicators (OPEN · WIN · LOSS · ACTIVE)
- Use `—` (em dash, no spaces) for punch: "Discipline. Not luck."
- Use short sentences. One idea per sentence.
- Use "operator" and "operate" as the primary framing
- Use "Risk Matrix Labs" as the full brand name — never "RML Dashboard" or "the Dashboard"
- Use $17/month (not "$17 per month" or "17 dollars a month")
- Use "sign up" (verb), "log in" (verb)

### DON'T
- Say "forever" for pricing claims → say "for as long as you stay subscribed"
- Say "like a professional operator" → just say "as an operator"
- Say "Supabase," "Vercel," or any infrastructure brand in user-facing copy
- Use exclamation marks in body copy (1 max per page, headline only)
- Use "easy" → say "straightforward" or just show the feature
- Use "revolutionary," "best," "world-class," "#1" without proof
- Use "gambler" as a positive term — it's always the thing we're moving away from
- Refer to the product as "Risk Matrix Dashboard" — it's "Risk Matrix Labs"

### Punctuation
- Oxford comma: Yes. ("Bankroll, risk, and discipline.")
- Em dash: No spaces. ("Operate — or don't.")
- Ellipsis: Avoid in marketing copy.

## Legal / Compliance Reminders

- Never guarantee results or wins
- "Forever" pricing claims → always qualify with "as long as you stay subscribed"
- Affiliate pages must include: "Affiliate links — RML may earn a commission. Must be 21+. Problem gambling? 1-800-GAMBLER."
- Earnings claims for affiliates → always include "results vary based on referred subscribers"
- Don't use "only" or "best" in comparative claims without backing
