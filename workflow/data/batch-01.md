**Tweet 1 (Hook):**
If you're betting the same dollar amount regardless of confidence level — you don't have a unit system. You have a habit.

Here's what actual unit sizing looks like. 🧵

**Tweet 2:**
A unit is a fixed % of your total bankroll.

Most disciplined operators use 1–3 units max per bet.

1 unit = base confidence
2 units = elevated edge
3 units = highest conviction only

You size up because the data supports it — not because you "feel good" about it.

**Tweet 3:**
Why this matters:

A bad 5-game stretch at flat 5% risk = -25% bankroll.

The same stretch at 1–2% risk = -8%.

The operator who survives the downswing stays in the game long enough to win it back. The other one reloads or quits.

**Tweet 4:**
Emotional sizing is what kills bankrolls.

Signs you're doing it wrong:
→ Betting bigger after a win "while you're hot"
→ Doubling a unit to "get it back"
→ No record of what unit you used or why

The bet log doesn't lie. The P&L curve doesn't lie.
